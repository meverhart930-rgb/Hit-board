Pick a date and build the board. Lineups firm up ~1–3 hours before first pitch — earlier than that you get projected pools, flagged on each card. Weather only loads for today and the next ~16 days.

Read this. This is a heuristic ranking score, not a true probability — it runs a few points hot, so use it to order hitters, not to price a bet. Contact skill drives most of the number; everything else is a nudge. Platoon and pitcher quality are the real signals. Batter-vs-pitcher samples are statistically meaningless at the sizes that exist and are weighted near zero. Day/night splits are shown but not scored — they're mostly noise. Weather barely affects hit frequency; its real use here is the rain-out flag, since a postponed game kills the bet. The single most important input is whether the player actually starts — cards are tagged Confirmed or Projected. Sportsbooks price hit props efficiently, so a high score usually means a known favorite, not value; the edge is in the mismatch the line missed, not the name at the top. Data: MLB Stats API + Open-Meteo (both free). Not betting advice.
